1.4. Expected value of a random variable

The expected value (or mean) represents the long-run average outcome of repeated experiments. It is a probability-weighted average.

For discrete variables:

\[ \begin{equation} \mathbb{E}[X]=\sum_x x\,\mathbb{P}(X=x) \end{equation} \]

For continuous variables:

\[ \begin{equation} \mathbb{E}[X]=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}x\,f(x)\,dx \end{equation} \]

The variance measures spread:

\[ \begin{equation} \mathrm{Var}(X)=\mathbb{E}\!\left[(X-\mathbb{E}[X])^2\right] \end{equation} \]
